1.Equity analysis of educational attainment by race for a Bay Area county (2019 5-yr data)

“stacked” bar charts “fill” bar charts From the two charts we can see that there is disproportionate education attainment by race in the county. It should be noted that the equity analysis based on the ACS 5-yr data where the education attainment statistics is just for “population 25 years or older”. Take White Alone for example. The proportion of white alone population with Bachelor’s degree or higher in the total population with Bachelor’s degree or higher is 56.4%. However, The proportion of white alone population in the total population is 52.8%. If education attainment were ‘race-blind’, these percentages would be the same, but instead, white alone population appear to be 6.4% (56.4%/52.8% - 1) more likely to have Bachelor’s degree or higher. In other words, white alone population are under-represented in terms of the education attainment (Bachelor’s degree or higher). Similarly, we can do the same computation for other races. In terms of the education attainment (Bachelor’s degree or higher), Black or African American population are over-represented by 66.7% (6.0%/3.6% - 1); American Indian and Alaska Native Alone population are over-represented by 160% (5.2‰/2.0‰ - 1); Asian Alone population are under-represented by 21.4% (33.5%/27.6% - 1); Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander Alone population are over-represented by 128% (5.7‰/2.5‰ - 1); Some Other Race Alone population are over-represented by 252% (8.8%/2.5% - 1); Two or More Races population are over-represented by 8.6% (3.8%/3.5% - 1);

“stacked” bar charts

“fill” bar charts From the two charts we can see that there is disproportionate education attainment by ethnicity in the county. It should be noted that the equity analysis based on the ACS 1-yr data where the education attainment statistics is also just for “population 25 years or older”.

As what we do in the first part, we can compute the under-represent or over-represent times. Here, I just show the results in terms of education attainment (Bachelor’s degree). White, not Latinx population are under-represented by 15.2% (48.5%/42.1% - 1); Latinx population are over-represented by 91.3% (19.7%/10.3% - 1); Not White, not Latinx population are under-represented by 8.1% (41.3%/38.2% - 1).

We can see the education attainment of Latinx should be paid attention to.

3.The over-representation of the Latinx 25+ population who have no college degree

From the chart we can see that the over/under-represention ratios Y (Y = [#Latinx pop with no college degree / # Total pop with no college degree] / [#Latinx pop / # Total pop]) are higher than 1.0 for any county in every year. This shows the eudcation attainment of Latinx population should be paid attention to. For county 041 (Marin County), Y are very high (more than 2.5) and Y are still increasing in recent years. This phenomenon should be attached great importance to by local government. For other counties, Y are relatively low. However, there seems no changes from 2006 to 2019. It is not bad trend. Something needs to be done to let Y decrease.
